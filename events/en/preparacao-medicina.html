Context: Getting Into Medicine in Portugal

Entry scores for Medicine sit between 182 and 189 points (on a scale of 200), depending on the faculty and the year. In 2024, ICBAS-UP recorded 184.7 and FMUL/UM hovered around 183-188 points.

The final score is a weighted average: typically 50% secondary school grades + 50% entrance exams (Biology and Geology, Physics and Chemistry A, Mathematics A).

There are roughly 1,300 to 1,500 places per year for the Integrated Master's in Medicine. Competition is fierce -- every tenth of a point counts.

A rise of 2 marks on an exam can mean the difference between getting in and being left out. That is precisely the gap this programme was designed to close.
